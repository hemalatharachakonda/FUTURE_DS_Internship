# Task 1 – Sales Data Analysis using Microsoft Excel

##  Internship
This project was completed as part of the **Future Interns Internship Program**.

##  Project Overview
The objective of this task was to analyze sales data using **Microsoft Excel** and generate meaningful insights using Excel features such as Pivot Tables, charts, and data aggregation.

The dataset contains sales information organized by **year and month**, allowing analysis of trends, comparison of performance, and identification of sales patterns.

##  Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Pivot Tables
- Charts (Bar Chart / Line Chart / Column Chart)
- Data Aggregation
- Basic Data Analysis

##  Dataset Details
The dataset includes:
- Year-wise sales data
- Month-wise sales data
- Total sales calculations
- Grand total of all sales

##  Analysis Performed
The following analysis was performed in Excel:

- Organized the raw sales data into a structured format
- Created Pivot Tables to summarize data by month and year
- Generated charts to visualize sales performance
- Calculated monthly sales totals
- Calculated yearly sales totals
- Calculated overall grand total
- Compared sales trends across months and years

##  Insights from Charts

**1. Which month recorded the highest sales?**  
From the chart analysis, one of the mid or end-of-year months recorded the highest sales, showing a strong demand period.

**2. Which month had the lowest sales?**  
The charts indicate that an early month in the dataset recorded the lowest sales, representing a slower business period.

**3. How do sales trends change across months?**  
Sales fluctuate across months, with some months performing better due to seasonal demand or market conditions.

**4. Which year shows the highest overall sales growth?**  
From the yearly comparison chart, the most recent year shows higher total sales, indicating growth compared to previous years.

**5. Are there seasonal patterns in sales?**  
Yes, the charts suggest that certain months consistently show higher sales, indicating possible seasonal demand patterns.

**6. How do monthly sales contribute to yearly totals?**  
Each month's performance contributes to the yearly total, and months with higher sales significantly increase the overall yearly revenue.

**7. Are there sudden spikes or drops in sales?**  
The charts reveal some spikes and dips in monthly sales, which may indicate promotional periods, seasonal demand, or business fluctuations.

**8. What does the grand total represent?**  
The grand total represents the overall sales generated across all months and years in the dataset.

##  Key Observations
- Sales performance varies across different months.
- Certain months contribute more significantly to yearly sales.
- Charts make it easier to visualize patterns and trends.
- Pivot tables simplify the analysis of large datasets.
