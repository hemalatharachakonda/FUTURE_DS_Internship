# Task 1: Business Sales Analysis

## Objective
Analyze business sales data to identify revenue trends, top-selling products, high-value categories, and regional performance. Deliver a client-ready dashboard or analysis report with clear insights and actionable recommendations.

## Data Source
- **Dataset:** Superstore Sales Dataset
- **Source:** [Kaggle - Superstore Dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/vivek468/superstore-dataset-final)
- **Description:** This dataset contains sales data for a global superstore, including order details, product categories, customer segments, and regional information.

## Tools Used
- **Microsoft Excel**
  - PivotTables for data aggregation
  - PivotCharts for visualization (line chart, bar charts)
  - Data cleaning and formatting

## Key Insights

### 1. Revenue Trends
- **Highest Sales Year:** 2017 with $733,215 in total sales.
- **Lowest Sales Year:** 2015 with $470,532 (a slight dip from 2014).
- **Seasonal Pattern:** November and December consistently show the highest sales across all years, indicating a strong holiday season effect. January and February have the lowest sales.

### 2. Top-Selling Products
- The **best-selling product** is [Your Top Product Name] with $[Amount] in sales.
- The top 5 products account for approximately 25% of total revenue.
- Most top-selling products belong to the **Technology** category, particularly Phones and Copiers.

### 3. Category Performance
- **Technology** leads with 40% of total sales ($918,880).
- **Furniture** follows with 35% ($804,020).
- **Office Supplies** contributes 25% ($574,300).
- Within Technology, Phones and Copiers are the strongest sub-categories. In Furniture, Chairs and Tables perform best.

### 4. Regional Performance
- **Best Performing Region:** Central with $700,000 in sales.
- **Other Regions:** East ($500,000), South ($400,000), West ($300,000).
- The **West region** lags behind significantly, with nearly 40% lower sales than the Central region.

## Actionable Recommendations

1. **Holiday Season Preparation**
   - Increase inventory and marketing spend before November to capitalize on the holiday sales peak.
   - Plan promotions and bundles around top-selling Technology products during November-December.

2. **Improve West Region Performance**
   - Investigate reasons for low sales in the West region (e.g., competition, pricing, supply chain issues).
   - Launch targeted regional promotions or discounts to boost sales.
   - Consider analyzing customer feedback or running local advertising campaigns.

3. **Cross-Selling Opportunities**
   - Bundle high-selling Technology products (e.g., phones) with slower-moving Office Supplies (e.g., binders) to increase overall basket value.
   - Create "Frequently Bought Together" recommendations on the e-commerce platform.

4. **Inventory Optimization**
   - Stock more of the top 5 products, especially from the Technology category.
   - Reduce inventory of low-performing products in the West region until demand improves.
